In the current 2026 landscape, ESRGAN is considered a "reliable workhorse," but it has been surpassed in two specific areas: structural consistency (avoiding that "ai-generated" look) and high-frequency detail (actual sharp textures instead of smooth plastic).

Here is how you can level up your architecture:

1. Upgrade the Core Model (The "What")
If you want to move beyond ESRGAN, you should look into Transformer-based or Attention-driven models. They "understand" the image context much better than pure CNNs, which helps prevent the fine details from getting "mushy."

SwinIR / HAT (Hybrid Attention Transformer): These are the current gold standards for open-source Python implementations. They use "Shifted Window" attention, which allows the model to look at the "big picture" while upscaling small tiles. This dramatically reduces the "seam" artifacts you might see in your tiling system.

Real-ESRGAN (v2+): If you stick with the GAN family, ensure you're using the "Real" variants. They use a "Pure Synthetic" degradation pipeline during training, which makes them much better at handling real-world noise and JPEG artifacts than the original ESRGAN.

2. [DONE] Move to "Frequency-Split" Blending (The "How")
Instead of a simple 0–100 alpha blend of the whole image, you can implement Laplacian Pyramid Blending.

Architecturally, you split the image into "High Frequencies" (edges, pores, fine hair) and "Low Frequencies" (color blobs, gradients, lighting).

The Element You're Missing: Keep 100% of the Low Frequency from your original resized image to ensure the lighting and colors stay perfect.

Apply your "Sharpness" slider only to the High Frequency layer generated by the AI. This prevents the "uncanny valley" look where the AI changes the lighting or "flattens" the depth of the scene.

3. [DONE] Edge-Aware Masking
Since you already have the "Detail Preservation" slider, you can make it "smart" by generating a Sobel or Canny mask of the original image.

The Strategy: Use the AI result heavily in "textured" areas (detected by the mask) but lean back on the original resized image for "flat" areas (like skin or sky).

ESRGAN is notorious for adding "noise" to flat areas where there shouldn't be any. An edge-aware mask keeps the sky clean while letting the AI sharpen the mountain.

4. Implementation Tip: The "Refiner" Pass
In professional pipelines (like those used in 2026's top-tier tools), we often use a two-stage process:

Stage 1 (Structure): A fast, conservative model (like a simple Bilinear or a light CNN) to set the "bones."

Stage 2 (Texture): Your ESRGAN or SwinIR model runs at a lower "denoising strength" over the Stage 1 output.
